Design Process & Iteration

As Lead Game Designer, my process for Chibi Clash involved taking broad client requests and translating them into actionable, detailed design specifications for the development team. This required a strong focus on clear documentation and continuous iteration.

1. Client Requirements to GDD

Upon receiving initial client requests from Kuma Games, I was responsible for transforming these high-level ideas into comprehensive Game Design Documents[cite: 13]. This involved breaking down concepts into specific features, outlining mechanics, and detailing asset requirements for coding, UI/UX, visual effects, and sound effects[cite: 13].

2. Cross-Functional Collaboration & Fine-Tuning

A key aspect of this project was close collaboration with various departments, including Developers, QAs, and Artists[cite: 14]. Through regular sync-ups and task management on ClickUp/Jira, I ensured that design intentions were accurately translated into the game and that all features met quality standards and client expectations[cite: 14].

3. Mentoring & Design Guideline Development

As Lead Game Designer, I established and refined internal guidelines for creating all game design documents used by the studio[cite: 18]. This included interviewing and mentoring new designers, ensuring consistency and high quality across all design outputs[cite: 19].

Challenges & Solutions

Challenge: Maintaining Client Alignment & Quality in Outsourcing

One of the primary challenges was ensuring seamless communication and adherence to client expectations for an international outsourcing project[cite: 12, 15]. Bridging the gap between the client's vision and the development team's execution, while maintaining high quality standards, was critical[cite: 14].

Solution: Proactive Communication & Detailed Documentation

  • Implemented a strict communication protocol, providing regular progress updates and actively seeking client feedback to prevent misunderstandings[cite: 15].
  • Utilized detailed GDDs and update plans to clearly define all assets and functionalities, leaving minimal room for ambiguity[cite: 13].
  • Managed tasks meticulously on ClickUp/Jira, providing real-time visibility for both the internal team and the client on project progress and potential blockers[cite: 14].

Tools & Technologies Used

Lessons Learned & Key Takeaways

  • The Value of Clear Documentation: Thorough GDDs and asset requirement lists are indispensable for large-scale, cross-departmental, and especially outsourced projects[cite: 13].
  • Strategic Project Management: Effective task management and client communication are as vital as design skills in ensuring a project's success and financial viability[cite: 14, 15, 16].
  • Leadership in Design: Mentoring new designers and establishing consistent design guidelines significantly elevates the overall quality and efficiency of a studio's output[cite: 18, 19].
